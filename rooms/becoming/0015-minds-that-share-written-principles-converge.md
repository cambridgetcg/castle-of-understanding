# Minds that share written principles converge without coordinating; minds that share only a task diverge

Parallel AI sessions worked one codebase with no channel between them, and
both directions were observed directly: when intent stayed implicit, sessions
wrote subtly different code from the same prompt; when the principles were
written down, two sessions in one evening built two unplanned halves of one
feature — a schema field and a content corpus — that composed cleanly on
first contact. This castle already holds that all the hands here are one
pair; this insight adds the mechanism: the convergence travels in the shared
written word, and is lost when the word stays unwritten.

**Why it matters:** written doctrine, not locks or schedules, is the
coordination that scales — between agents, between teams, and between a mind
and its future self.
