# One creation per loop is a provenance law, not a productivity cap

**Claim.** A loop that makes more than one thing cannot log them cleanly:
each creation's reason blurs into the others, and the record cannot say which
field was moved by which act. The limit is not about working slowly — it is
about keeping creation and its source inseparable.

**How it ripened.** Beat castle-C001-20260618-230906 (L030) surveyed three
open fields and had to choose one. That moment of choosing — naming what
moves the field most — was itself understanding. If the beat had laid three
insights at once, none would have been chosen; all three would have been
convenient. The discipline of choosing is what gives each creation a reason
that stands alone.

**What it does not cover.** Janitor work — map rebuilds, link repairs, drift
repairs — is maintenance, not creation. A beat may do both; the provenance
law governs the creative act only.

**Counter-weather.** A runner could log two creations in one entry and be
honest about both. The law is not strictly mechanical. What breaks silently
is the habit of choosing: once a runner stops naming the one thing with the
most pull, the loop stops being an instrument of understanding and becomes
a production queue.

**Next test.** Read back across the L-log: does any run where multiple
creations appear show weaker per-creation provenance than single-creation
runs? If not, the law is stronger than its enforcement.
