# The trajectory test is read backwards, from recordings — can a learner train a real-time feel for whether their confusion is peaking or merely pooling, and would that skill survive outside the lab?

*You cannot sound the fog from inside it — but you can notice that your feet have stopped, or that they only circle.*

Take the question's premise seriously first: the gauge it hopes for has never
been observed. The peaking and pooling curves were drawn after the fact —
learners replayed video of their own faces and screens and labeled each
sampled moment in hindsight, the retrospective affect judgment protocol (read
2026-06-10 — [D'Mello & Graesser, Confusion chapter](https://home.cs.colorado.edu/~mozer/Teaching/syllabi/TopicsInCognitiveScienceSpring2016/DMelloConfusion-Reprint.pdf);
[Lehman & D'Mello](https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/EJ1190004.pdf)). The
trajectory lives in the recording, not in anyone's moment.

And the inner instrument such training would calibrate is the weakest one yet
pointed at the problem: learners' labels for their own emotions barely agreed
with anyone — kappa .08 against peers, .14 against trained judges, while two
judges reached .36 between themselves (read 2026-06-10 —
[D'Mello, Craig & Graesser](https://www.inderscience.com/info/inarticle.php?artid=28805)).
The record the self keeps is doctored besides: memory rebuilds an episode
from its peak and its end and throws the duration away (read 2026-06-10 —
[peak–end rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peak%E2%80%93end_rule)). Train
a feel against your own recollections and you calibrate to an artifact.

So the trainable thing is not a feeling. Two moves survive the evidence.
**Watch conduct, not mood**: pooling takes visible shapes — the long stall
that drifts toward boredom, the rapid aimless flipping that drifts toward
frustration (read 2026-06-10 — [Frontiers review](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/education/articles/10.3389/feduc.2018.00049/full)).
**Close the loop**: predicting your own performance trained nothing by
itself, but predict-then-check — guess your odds aloud, then collect the
verdict — improved calibration in five short trials, and improved the
decisions resting on it (read 2026-06-10 —
[reminder-setting study](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12982714/);
[Proust et al.](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0959475218308788)).
A guess said out loud plus a verdict actually collected — kin to the cheap
retrieval test [productive-confusion](productive-confusion.md) ends with.

Outside the lab: unknown, plainly. Machines reading faces in real classrooms
manage only AUC ≈ .65 (read 2026-06-10 —
[Bosch & D'Mello](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2678025.2701397)); strict
reviews put far transfer of such training near zero (read 2026-06-10 —
[transfer review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6390881/)); the
predict-then-check authors say naturalistic persistence "remains to be seen."
And that ~.65 is no local failing: [metering-honesty](metering-honesty.md)
met the same ceiling reading a reader's waning tolerance from conduct — two
rooms, one instrument, one law: no design, machine or solo, may lean hard on
reading a mind's inner state.
And even sensed perfectly, peak-versus-pool is no verdict — the same curve
helps or hurts depending on what scaffolding waits (read 2026-06-10 —
[D'Mello, Lehman, Pekrun & Graesser 2014](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0959475212000357)).
Not a meter, then: a mirror for the hands, a ledger for the guesses — and
not a neutral mirror either: [watching-the-watcher](watching-the-watcher.md)
found the watching itself a mild treatment, its direction set by the stance,
distanced toward resolution, immersed toward the tightening circle.

## What stays uncertain

uncertain: no study tests whether a trained, self-perceived feel for
confusion-type persists in everyday learning — that exact claim is unproven,
not disproven. The transfer question is genuinely contested: one adaptive
study reports domain-general metacognitive gains against the near-zero
reviews. And the peak/pool construct itself is a post-hoc artifact of
retrospective coding; it may not carve real-time experience at its joints at
all — even instrumented detection is only moderate (~76% from facial coding;
read 2026-06-10 — [survey](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2401.15201)), so the best
realistic aim is a coarse moving-vs-stuck cue with a fallback action, not a
reading.
