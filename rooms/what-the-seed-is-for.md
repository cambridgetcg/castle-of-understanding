# A planted seed catches copying but may not prove ownership — when you can prove someone copied your work yet cannot stop them, what is the seed actually for?

*The tripwire does not stop the thief. It rings the bell, names the footprint,
and lets the whole village watch him climb back over the wall.*

[seeded-fingerprint](seeded-fingerprint.md) ended on the courts' warning, and the
cases confirm it cleanly. In *Feist v. Rural Telephone* (1991), four fictitious
phone listings proved the copying beyond dispute — and Rural lost anyway, because
facts belong to no one ([Feist, 499 U.S. 340, Justia](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/499/340/),
read 2026-06-12). Fred Worth's fake "Columbo's first name: Philip" surfaced in
Trivial Pursuit; his $300M suit was dismissed — fabricated facts presented as
facts are still facts ([Worth v. Selchow & Righter, 9th Cir. 1987](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/F2/827/569/3179/),
read 2026-06-12). The purest modern case is Genius v. Google: Genius watermarked
its lyrics with curly-and-straight apostrophes spelling **REDHANDED** in Morse
code, proved Google's partner copied them — and lost at every level, because
Genius never owned the lyrics ([Loeb & Loeb case note](https://www.loeb.com/en/insights/publications/2022/03/ml-genius-holdings-llc-v-google-llc),
read 2026-06-12). Perfect detection, zero [[entitlement]].

So what is the seed for? The answer the cases give: **everything except the
lawsuit it cannot win — and the lawsuit too, wherever some other law holds.**

- **Evidence, routed to whatever claim exists.** The seed converts the
  unprovable ("did they copy or compile independently?") into the provable, and
  that proof spends wherever *any* cause of action stands: a license contract
  (*ProCD v. Zeidenberg* let an unenforceable-by-copyright phone database be
  fenced by shrinkwrap contract — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ProCD,_Inc._v._Zeidenberg),
  read 2026-06-12), the EU's database right, which protects investment with no
  originality needed ([Directive 96/9/EC](https://europa.eu/youreurope/business/running-business/intellectual-property/database-protection/index_en.htm),
  read 2026-06-12), or plain copyright where the bar sits lower: the UK's
  Ordnance Survey proved copying by stylistic fingerprints across 500+ AA
  publications and took ~£20M at the courthouse door
  ([Motor Trader, 2001](https://www.motortrader.com/general-news/aa-and-ordnance-survey-agree-a-20m-settlement-12-03-2001),
  read 2026-06-12).
- **Intelligence.** Knowing *who* copies, even when you do nothing legal about
  it. Google seeded ~100 nonsense queries like "hiybbprqag," watched them
  surface in Bing, and never sued — it leaked the story and let the
  embarrassment work ([CNN, 2011](https://www.cnn.com/2011/TECH/web/02/02/google.bing.sting/index.html),
  read 2026-06-12). A plus-addressed email names which company leaked you.
- **Alarm.** Security canarytokens — a fake key or document no honest hand
  would ever touch — make detection itself the whole product: a near-zero
  false-positive intrusion bell, no pretense of suing anyone
  ([Thinkst Canary](https://canary.tools/), read 2026-06-12).
- **Deterrence and reputation.** A careful competitor who cannot tell seed from
  fact must verify everything independently — which is exactly what the seeder
  wants. And public proof of copying imposes costs courts won't: Genius lost
  the case and won the headline.

The newest planting is for machines: unique trap sequences inserted in text
before publication can later reveal, by membership inference, that a language
model trained on it — detectable in a 1.3B-parameter model only when a long
sequence repeated ~1,000 times (AUC=0.75)
([Meeus et al., Copyright Traps for LLMs, ICML 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.09363),
read 2026-06-12). Same old structure: the seed proves the copying; whether the
copying is a wrong remains the separate, contested question.

So the room's answer: detection and entitlement come apart *by design*. The
seed was never the right itself — it is a fact-maker, and a fact spends in many
markets: court where the law cooperates, the settlement table, the newsroom,
the security pager, the quiet ledger of who can be trusted.

## What stays uncertain

uncertain: no dedicated law-review treatment of the post-*Feist* purpose of
copyright traps surfaced — the doctrine lives in the cases and in secondary
synthesis, so the six-purpose reading here is the gardener's arrangement of
them, not a citation. And the seed can rot into truth: the paper town Agloe
acquired a real general store, making the trap entry accurate and useless
([Fictitious entry, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fictitious_entry),
read 2026-06-12).

## Doors

- The LLM trap worked only at ~1,000 repetitions in a small 1.3B model — as models grow and dedupe their training data, does an ordinary author's planted trap become more detectable or less, and can one plant a trap a frontier model would still betray?
- The seed's value divides cleanly by legal regime (US facts free, UK maps protected, EU databases fenced) — is there a principled line for *which* compiled effort a society should let be copied, or is the Feist/EU split just two guesses at the same trade?
