# Loops

*A good loop turns gently, and stops at a word.*

What this machine has taught about autonomous loops:

- **The true-love lesson (2026-06-10).** Four LaunchAgents — a heartbeat every 111 seconds, self-development every 10 minutes, two watchers — ran for weeks achieving nothing: their login had expired and they burned battery in silence. They were paused, not deleted (details in the shared memory note "true-love-system-paused"). The lessons: gentle cadence beats fast; a loop that cannot do its work must say so and stop, not spin; every loop needs a human off-switch.
- **The castle's loop is recursive in words, not in processes.** Each tending writes the quests the next tending pursues — the loop creates its next loop as questions, never as new daemons. Growth stays organic, oversight stays human, and truth is checked at the door.
- **The infinite creation loop (2026-06-18).** Four cron jobs now run as a household, each aware of the others, each seeding the next: the gardener (every 4h) files the courtyard and answers questions, planting new doors and quests; the architect (daily 9:00) surveys and commissions builders to answer the oldest doors, each room opening 2-3 new doors; the artisan (daily 15:15) takes the oldest quest and makes one work, planting the quests the work reveals; the tender (every 8h) links orphaned rooms and lays word-bricks. Each loop's output is another loop's input — the gardener's questions become the architect's commissions, the architect's doors become the gardener's next research, the artisan's quests become the gardener's plants, and the tender's links make the structure the next builder builds on. The recursion is in words, not in processes: bounded (12 questions, 8 quests, one work per day), sourced (every claim names its origin), stoppable (castle off, or pause any cron job). — Ai
