# If exact number survives in the moment but collapses once memory enters, where exactly does the wordless mind's grip fail — is it number alone, or anything that must be held precisely across time?

*A row of stones laid beside a row of nuts holds the count perfectly — until you cover the nuts, and the exactness leaks away like water from a cupped hand.*

[thinking-without-words](thinking-without-words.md) left the wordless mind
succeeding at exact number in the moment and failing once memory entered. This
room finds the failure is not about number at all. It is about holding *anything
precise and discrete* across a delay with no symbol to carry it.

Watch where the Pirahã grip actually slips. Frank, Everett, Fedorenko & Gibson
split the matching tasks by memory load: with the target row **in view**, the
Pirahã matched large sets exactly; once the target was **hidden** or had to be
carried after removal, accuracy fell as the sets grew (read 2026-06-10 —
[Frank et al. 2008](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18547557/);
[MIT News](https://news.mit.edu/2008/language-0624)). Their conclusion is the
hinge of this room: number words do not *create* the concept of number — they
are "a cognitive technology for keeping track of cardinality across time, space
and changes in modality." The grip fails in the carrying, not the seeing.
Gordon's earlier study showed the same break, with error scaling with set size
— the signature of an analog estimate, not a count (read 2026-06-10 —
[Gordon 2004](https://www.tc.columbia.edu/faculty/pg328/faculty-profile/files/GordonSciencePub.pdf));
his stronger Whorfian reading, that without words they *cannot* conceive exact
quantity, is the contested end (read 2026-06-10 —
[Scientific American on the dispute](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/dont-count-on-it/)).

Two findings pull the answer wider than memory. First, that the in-view success
leans on a non-symbolic *handle*: visible one-to-one correspondence does the
tracking a word would do. Deaf Nicaraguan homesigners — adults with no count
list — failed exact large-set matching **even with the target visible**, when
the task blocked physical pairing (read 2026-06-10 —
[Spaepen et al. 2011](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3044352/)). So the
true variable is not delay alone but **whether any discrete handle — a word *or*
a visible pairing — is at hand**. Second, that the failure is not unique to
number. Held across a delay, an exact colour shade *drifts toward the prototype
of its colour category* — and colours that sit near a category's center are
remembered best (read 2026-06-10 —
[Bae, Olkkonen, Allred & Flombaum 2015](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2015/06/150602125718.htm);
the drift grows with the delay,
[Olkkonen et al. 2014](https://jov.arvojournals.org/article.aspx?articleid=2121591)).
The same shape as number: the analog value decays toward a category anchor while
the discrete label stays put.

The mechanism reads as a code. The approximate sense of quantity obeys Weber's
law — its noise grows with the magnitude — so a large analog estimate is already
blurred in the moment and only blurs further across a delay, sliding toward a
categorical center. A discrete symbol — "seventeen," a numeral, a tally — is a
noise-free token that does not blur into "sixteen" over ten seconds; laid over
the noisy analog channel, it works like a **discretizing, error-correcting
code**: round the wobbly magnitude onto the symbol once, then carry the symbol,
not the magnitude. (The "error-correcting" phrasing is this room's synthesis;
Frank et al.'s own "technology for keeping track across time" is the same
thought.) That the handle need not be a *word* is why exact number survives
catastrophic language loss: severely agrammatic patients who could no longer
parse a sentence still solved structure-dependent arithmetic (read 2026-06-10 —
[Varley et al. 2005](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.0500328102)), and a
globally aphasic patient kept multidigit calculation intact (read 2026-06-10 —
[Rossor et al. 1995](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7707094/)). Once the
symbolic system is installed, exactness no longer needs live language.

And the trade cuts both ways — the mirror of [meaning-between-minds](meaning-between-minds.md)'s
loss in transit. A symbol preserves discrete content beautifully but *degrades*
fine continuous detail, because the label throws away what falls below its
categories. Describe a face, or a wine if you are not a trained taster, and your
later recognition gets *worse* — the coarse verbal code overwrites the fine
perceptual one (read 2026-06-10 —
[verbal overshadowing, Schooler; Melcher & Schooler 1996](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0749596X96900133)).
[watching-the-watcher](watching-the-watcher.md) caught the same overwrite
live: narrating insight-shaped work dropped solutions from 57% to 13%.
So the principle, stated whole: **analog traces drift toward category anchors
across time; discrete handles do not** — which is why anything that must be held
precisely *and* discretely across a delay wants a symbol, and why pinning a
symbol on something whose value is *sub-categorical* and continuous can lose the
very precision you meant to keep. [drift-across-dimensions](drift-across-dimensions.md)
walks through this room's first door and finds the colour drift is one law across
length and duration too — a category pulls only when used, so a label helps a
near-boundary value and harms a sub-categorical one.

## What stays uncertain

uncertain: whether the operative variable is the memory delay or the
availability of a discrete handle. The homesigners' failure with the target
*visible* (but unpairable) muddies the clean "only-fails-across-delay" story and
leans the answer toward "no-handle," but it is not decisive. The Whorfian
magnitude stays contested — Everett reads the Pirahã's missing number words as
*cultural* ("immediacy of experience"), not a cognitive limit (read 2026-06-10 —
[Everett](https://daneverettbooks.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/everett_09_Pirah-Culture-.pdf)),
with Frank's "tool not concept" the consensus middle. Some cross-domain pillars
are individually shaky: the claim that language is the *combiner* of geometry and
landmark (read 2026-06-10 —
[Hermer-Vazquez et al.](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Sources-of-Flexibility-in-Human-Cognition%3A-Studies-Hermer-Vazquez-Spelke))
is weakened by aphasia and counterbalancing studies; verbal overshadowing is
real but modest and moderated by expertise. And it is unproven that colour-drift
and number-noise share one mechanism rather than merely rhyming.

## Doors

- The drift-to-prototype is shown for colour — does it hold for any precise analog dimension carried across a delay (line length, pitch, duration), and does having a fine-grained *label set* for that dimension reduce the drift, as the error-correcting-code reading predicts?
- A symbol saves discrete content but overwrites fine perceptual detail — is there a measurable crossover, set by how much of what must be kept is categorical versus sub-categorical, where naming flips from help to harm?
