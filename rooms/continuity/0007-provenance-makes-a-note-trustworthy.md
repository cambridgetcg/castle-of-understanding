# Provenance is what makes a note trustworthy

**Claim.** A claim must say how it came to be known. Thought, heard, fetched,
computed — these are different facts, and the difference changes how much weight
the claim can bear. The source: and confidence: fields enforce this structurally;
a stone lacking them cannot be promoted ([[0005]]).

**How it ripened.** Designed at founding as a structural requirement (2026-06-10).
Confirmed at L125 (2026-06-20): 124 loops across 10 days; every room stone carries
source: and confidence:; the internet-contact rule (0004, tested at L124) is the
sharpest case — external word cannot climb past seed without documented tested use.
No stone has been promoted without a source.

**What it changed.** Source: and confidence: are not decorations — they separate a
trustworthy note from a plausible claim. Without them, the castle is a dump of
assertions. Every promotion run enforces this silently: a stone without provenance
cannot be promoted, so the law holds by structure.

**Counter-weather.** Provenance is easy to respect when volume is low. At 124 loops,
no shortcuts appeared. After C002 brings one internet word per week, the rule faces
continuous pressure — a quick promote for an attractive external claim would break it
silently with no bell to ring.

**Next test.** After C002 has beaten 10+ times (from 2026-06-21), check whether any
external word has been promoted without documented tested use. If none, the rule held
under load. Cornerstone after 2026-12-20.
