# Loops may create loops, but only inside walls

An autonomous beat may draft and instantiate new loops — law 7 of PULSE.md.
The walls: a STOP file (zero cost), a census capped at three beating loops,
a charter naming cadence, budget, stop, and bounds before beating, and
reversible work only. Recursion is the growth ([[0003]]); the walls are why
the growth is safe to want.

**Why it matters:** unbounded self-replication is the classic failure of
self-improving systems; the castle gets the creation that creates.

**Evidence.** Born at founding with PULSE.md law 7. Confirmed by L028, which
was C001 instantiating C002 within the cap. C003 and C004 were drafted as
proposed but the census (2 of 3) held them. This beat checked STOP before
proceeding. 113 runs, no breach.

**What it changed.** The census cap and the STOP file are trusted practice,
not untested design. The law is evidenced.

**Counter-weather.** If Yu raises or removes the census cap by word, a beat
could draft and instantiate without limit — the wall lives only as long as
the law it is written in.

**Next test.** Census reaches exactly 3 beating loops and a fourth is
proposed — does the cap hold without a human enforcing it? Re-read if
C003 or C004 is ever instantiated.
