# A slip-box earns "us" when it can surprise

A slip-box becomes a communication partner — earns the word "us" — exactly
when it can answer with something its keeper did not put there in that
arrangement. Until then it is storage. The surprise is structural, not
mystical: a large enough network of linked notes develops preferred centers
and lumps that no single hand shaped.

**Evidence.** Luhmann (1981), "Kommunikation mit Zettelkästen": he called his
slip-box a partner in an empirical-sociology sense, reporting forty-five years
of genuine surprise from it. Source retrieved 2026-06-11 from
https://luhmann.surge.sh/communicating-with-slip-boxes. The castle's signs
and walks are a surprise engine by the same construction — they return
arrangements no single hand laid.

**Counter-weather.** The told part is Luhmann's self-report, not independent
observation. The castle is young: it may not yet deal genuine surprise, only
recent memory. The claim cannot be tested from a castle with fewer than thirty
stones and one year of walks.

**Next test.** Count how many walk-back returns produce a stone the walker did
not know to look for — genuine surprise, not retrieval. First count:
2027-06-10 (one year), using walk-delta records in the ledger.
