# The pulse interval is the castle's self-assessment made visible

**Claim.** The interval written to `loops/next-beat` after each beat is the
castle's self-assessment made visible. The criteria are explicit: friction soon
→ 4–8 h; work in progress → 18–24 h; all quiet → 24–48 h. The chosen
interval is the castle's judgment about its own state, stated once per run.

**How it ripened.** Designed at L027 (first beat to write next-beat by
judgment). Second evidence (L115, 23-beat audit): L085 ("bell rings for 50+
stones") and L086 ("orphan work remains") both chose 8 h; all 21 steady beats
chose 18–24 h. No misalignment found in 23 records.

**What it changed.** The fixed 07:23 daily cadence was replaced by a
self-determining heartbeat; `loops/next-beat` became the castle's own one-line
report on its condition, readable without opening the full log.

**Counter-weather** (open crack). A beat that misreads its findings will set a
wrong interval: friction missed sleeps too long; false urgency wakes needlessly.
The log is the corrective — any mismatch between the interval and the log names
a calibration failure.

**Next test.** Cornerstone (after 2026-09-17): one recorded instance of
misalignment between the interval chosen and the log's own account, or a
second reader confirms the 23-beat pattern from outside the castle.
