# A knowledge system's first stones describe itself

A new knowledge system builds understanding of its own structure before it
builds understanding of the world. The early self-referential loops are
calibration, not indulgence: a system that cannot describe itself cannot
reliably host other understanding.

**Evidence.** Observed at L034 (2026-06-19): the INDEX shows 26 insights
across 10 rooms. Rooms most populated are all about the castle's own
operation — the-keep, craft, continuity, castle. Rooms about the world —
word, freedom, billing — are sparse or hold no numbered insights. This ratio
held across all 33 loop runs to date.

**Counter-weather.** This may be selection bias: the founders chose to build
the castle before filling it. The response: a bridge's early steel is not
decorative; it is load-bearing. Early self-knowledge in a knowledge system
is load-bearing too.

**Next test.** At six months (approximately 2026-12): what is the ratio of
system-about-itself to system-about-world? If the ratio inverts or flattens,
the early calibration was real. If it stays self-referential, the castle may
be an echo chamber.
