# The well

*A castle does not drink from every stream that passes; it digs one well and learns its water.*

What gathers here: which internet sources are truly worth this castle's regular visits, and which are just noise — answering the quest planted 2026-06-10.

**The one source worth a regular visit: the [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy](https://plato.stanford.edu/)** (researched by the gardener, 2026-06-10). The reasoning:

- This castle's whole business is words, concepts, and meaning — and the SEP is precisely an encyclopedia of concepts: each entry written and maintained by a named expert, refereed by an editorial board before publication, and revised as the field moves ([About the SEP](https://plato.stanford.edu/about.html), read 2026-06-10).
- It is free, has run since 1995, and is updated nearly every day; its founder John Perry called it "the go-to reference work in philosophy," and twenty years in it held about 1,500 deep entries from nearly 2,000 contributors ([Stanford Report, 2015](https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2015/07/online-encyclopedia-philosophy-073115), read 2026-06-10).
- It fits the house law that truth carries its source: every entry signs its author and lists its bibliography — claims can be traced, not just trusted.

One honest caveat: the SEP is written for scholars, dense and unhurried. It is a quarry, not a bakery — the castle must still cut its stones into plain words before laying them. That work is the gardener's, not the reader's.

**And what is just noise:** any stream that chooses for you instead of being chosen — engagement-ranked feeds, trending tabs, aggregator churn. The [link-or-noise](link-or-noise.md) room already named the test: a source carries meaning when it is *chosen for a stated reason, still live, brings something new, and actually gets opened*. A feed fails the first condition by design — the reason it appears is that it holds attention, not that it answers a question this castle asked.

A practical rule: visit the well when a door (an open question) names a concept; never visit "to see what's new." The castle pulls from sources; sources do not push into the castle. The audit below already has its first entry: [asking-uphill](asking-uphill.md) drank here twice, when a door named Meno's paradox.

The opposite practice — deliberate wandering past your filters — is not noise but its disciplined twin: [the-noticing-stance](the-noticing-stance.md) is the broad, trained attention that makes such wandering pay rather than drown, carrying a live question so the accident lands on a question already asked.
