# The two meters fail together — alexithymia leaves a person blind to both feeling and pulse. Is interoception trainable, so the missing backup channel can be built where it is weakest, or is the doubly-blind state fixed?

*A path worn into grass — walk it daily and it holds; stop, and it greens back over. And the ground you most need to cross takes a path least.*

Check the premise first. The question assumes the person is truly *blind to
pulse* — but on measured ability that half wobbles. Meta-analyses find no
reliable link between alexithymia and objective heartbeat detection; alexithymia
tracks what you *believe* about your body, not what you can *detect* ([PMC, read
2026-06-10](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11542822/)). The dominant
test, heartbeat counting, is confounded by what you already know about your own
heart rate — across 133 studies and some 11,500 people it showed no significant
tie to alexithymia at all ([Collabra, read
2026-06-10](https://online.ucpress.edu/collabra/article/8/1/33271/121638/How-Does-Heartbeat-Counting-Task-Performance)).
The second meter may be more poorly *measured* than truly dark.

Where a real deficit exists, can the channel be built? Short-term, yes. One
session of cardiac biofeedback lifts heartbeat accuracy with a large effect
([PMC, read 2026-06-10](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9124832/)); an
eight-week body-scan raises accuracy and confidence over idle controls
([Frontiers, read
2026-06-10](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/human-neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2017.00452/full)).
The strongest evidence is a 285-person trial: mindfulness and partner-based
practice both lowered alexithymia and raised interoceptive awareness, and the
awareness gains statistically *mediated* the alexithymia drop — the channel
looks like part of the mechanism, not a bystander ([ScienceDirect, read
2026-06-10](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0165032723010704)).
After training, the anterior insula, the brain's interoceptive hub, strengthens
its inward links — though that picture comes from seventeen people with no
control group ([PMC, read
2026-06-10](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11116496/)).

But the gains are fragile, slow, and uneven. Over three weekly sessions the
first bump did not accumulate or persist ([Frontiers, read
2026-06-10](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/neuroscience/articles/10.3389/fnins.2022.838055/full));
control groups improve too, so plain practice can masquerade as training ([PMC,
read 2026-06-10](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7076086/)). In a
nine-month course, objective accuracy moved only after six to nine months
([PubMed, read 2026-06-10](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27925645/)). And
*build it where it is weakest* is the least-supported reading: in patients with
genuinely low accuracy, training eased distress but did not beat a waiting
control on heartbeat perception ([PubMed, read
2026-06-10](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25038304/)) — even while one
body-scan study found gains largest in low starters. Worst of all, confidence
rises without accuracy: feeling more attuned is no proof the channel got built.

Not fixed — but no clean fix. Nudgeable, fading, slow, and least willing exactly
where it is needed most.

## What stays uncertain

uncertain: whether *interoception* is even one channel to rebuild. The argument
in *There is no such thing as interoception* is that cardiac, respiratory and
gut perception correlate weakly and training one does not transfer to the others
— if so, there is no single backup channel, only narrow skills ([Frontiers, read
2026-06-10](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1488415/full)).
The alexithymia-to-accuracy tie is quadratic, not a clean deficit: very high
alexithymia can pair with high *or* low detection ([PMC, read
2026-06-10](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7076086/)), so no one
prescription fits all. And more monitoring is not always safe — for
anxiety-prone people, attention to bodily signals can feed catastrophic
misreading.

## Doors

- The partner-based practice beat solo mindfulness at building the inner sense and lowering alexithymia — why does another body in the room train interoception better than turning attention inward alone, and does it need a real partner or only a responsive other?
- Training raises felt confidence without raising accuracy, and for anxious people more body-attention can harm — could the accuracy channel be built while the felt confidence is held back, so attunement never outruns the skill?
- If there is no single interoceptive channel — only cardiac, respiratory and gut skills that don't transfer — which one is the right backup for flooding, when the flood reads as a racing pulse but breath is the channel a person can actually steer?
