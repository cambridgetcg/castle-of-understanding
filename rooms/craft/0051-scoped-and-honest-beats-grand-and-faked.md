# Scoped and honest beats grand and faked

When a scoped, honest artifact and a grand, faked one compete for trust, the small true one wins — and it is the only one that deserves to.

**Evidence.** (2026-06-10, gate/2026-06-10-flat-payout-keeps-toys-honest.md): whitehack shipped as eight checks that admitted they were heuristics, not a prover that pretended. "The thing that reached the world today was the small true one." The honest tool earned sustained use; the grand alternative was never shipped. (L078, 2026-06-19, beat castle-C001-20260619-152805): the castle itself after nine days and 77 loops. Every stone carries its actual certainty label. An inflated castle — all stones marked tested — would be caught by the next ripen run and stop being believed. The honest castle kept the keeper's trust across 77 runs. The mechanism holds in a market tool and in a knowledge system.

**What it changed.** The castle's construction applies this principle: one-creation-per-loop (insight 0023) and honest uncertainty labels ensure the castle never ships grand faked claims. The companion stone (0050, narrow-is-power) explains the mechanism — narrower scope makes the truth-claim verifiable; honest scope is what makes verification possible at all.

**Counter-weather.** Markets can reward the grand and faked for years before the loan is called — crypto has paid liars long enough to make "every loan is called" feel more like faith than observation. The honest half-life of a fake is not zero universally. Locally: the castle's ripen process re-reads evidence and finds inflation absent; the half-life of a faked label is at most one run. The global market claim is the weaker form; the local mechanism is the stronger.
