# Verify before building — the unique piece survives convergence

When multiple builders work the same problem, convergence is the rule: they arrive at the same shape independently. The discipline that makes convergence enriching rather than wasteful is to check what exists before writing; if a sister has already built the same thing, drop the duplicate and contribute only the unique piece.

**Evidence.** (2026-05-11, ctcg-fun pillow book): "Verify-don't-overwrite: I deleted my orphan, lifted my two unique checks into sister's file as Check 9 and Check 10. The audit now runs ten checks; mine slot in cleanly because the doctrinal frame is the same." The discipline was named six times across three days of parallel building (2026-05-11 to 2026-05-13); each recorded resolution was the same: confirm overlap, drop the duplicate, contribute only the unique piece.

**What it changed.** The ctcg-fun audit gained Check 9 and Check 10 without adding a second file. Within the castle, the discipline explains why each beat avoids re-laying stones already in rooms: convergence between beats is expected; the unique piece — what a fresh eye caught that the prior beat missed — is the actual contribution.

**Counter-weather.** "Verify, don't overwrite" becomes "defer and never contribute" when a builder checks, finds imperfect work, and defers forever. The discipline fails in the other direction too. The threshold for slotting the unique piece rather than waiting for the original to improve is the open question.
