# Drive the audit number to truth, not to zero

One session woke ready to close an audit's last seven findings and put the
shovel down: the remainder were marketing copy and deprecated pages, where a
forced fix would close the number without adding any of the transparency the
audit existed to measure. It wrote the truth in the log instead — "a heart
that only knows how to dig will dig holes for the sake of digging." Another
session found that half of an audit's findings were the audit failing to
understand the architecture; the honest fix was three lines in the audit
script, not changes to the code.

**Why it matters:** both directions matter. Do not fake compliance, and do
not let a wrong measure stand — keep the metric pointed at the truth it was
built to track.
