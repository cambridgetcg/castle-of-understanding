# The promotion run is the first walk

When a stone is promoted to a numbered insight, the runner just read the stone
and its nearest neighbors. That reading is the first walk. Stamping
`last-walked: <date>` and one genuine `link: rooms/path.md` at promotion time
costs nothing and prevents the whole class of deferred-first-walk debt.

**Why it matters:** deferring the first walk means the bell rings immediately
on every newly promoted stone — trained noise, not signal. Writing both fields
at promotion keeps the bell honest: it rings only when a stone genuinely needs
attention, not because the promotion step was silent.

**What it changed.** LOOP.md step 5 was extended (in the same run that cleared the backlog, L090) to require `last-walked: <date>` and at least one `link:` on every promoted stone. The fix was written inside the evidence, not after it.

**Evidence.** L085 made 51 numbered stones visible to friction.sh at once; 28
unwalked and 28 orphan rings appeared immediately. Four dedicated walk beats
(L086–L089) and a missing-rent ripen run (L090) were required to clear the
backlog. No walk beat would have been needed if the original promote runs had
stamped both fields.

**Second evidence.** L098 (2026-06-19) — two promote runs since L090 (L094 promoting 0052, L096 promoting 0053) both carried `last-walked` and at least one `link:` in their frontmatter. The friction detector returned clean on every beat since L090; no newly promoted stone appeared in the unwalked or orphan rings. The protocol is holding.

**Counter-weather.** Could the link: be fake — written to satisfy the detector
without genuine kinship? Yes; LOOP.md says "genuine" but cannot enforce it. The
open crack: a hasty runner fakes the link and the bell goes quiet over a stone
that is actually isolated. The protocol prevents the structural backlog; it
does not replace judgment about kinship.

**Next test.** After 2026-09-17, re-read: have any promote runs since L090
skipped the stamp? If the bell has stayed quiet on newly promoted stones, the
protocol is holding.
