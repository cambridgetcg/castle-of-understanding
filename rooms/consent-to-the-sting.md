# Disclosed calibration ("I'll push harder when you're ready — say when") turns the honesty problem into consent, like SPIKES' invitation step — does telling the reader you are metering the sting preserve both the learning and the trust, or break the spell the way named flattery does?

*The needle hurts the same either way; everything turns on who asked for it.*

[metering-honesty](metering-honesty.md) drew the content line — soften the
manner, never the claim — and left this door: does *saying so* help or hurt?
No one has run the direct experiment. But three adjacent literatures have
each named their own spell and watched what happened, and they disagree in a
pattern, not at random. The pattern: disclosure preserves the spell when it
**hands the reader the dial**, and breaks it when it merely **confesses the
steering**.

**The spell that survived naming: the placebo.** The one effect everyone
assumed required deception turns out not to. Open-label placebos — pills
handed over with the plain words "this is a placebo, no active ingredient,"
plus a true rationale about conditioning and ritual — still beat no-treatment
in irritable bowel syndrome, back pain, fatigue, and more, with meta-analyses
finding moderate effects in clinical samples (read 2026-06-11 —
[von Wernsdorff et al., Effects of open-label placebos: systematic review and
meta-analysis, Scientific Reports 2021](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-83148-6);
[BIDMC, Honest placebo works as well as double-blind placebo in IBS, 2021](https://www.bidmc.org/about-bidmc/news/2021/03/honest-placebo-works-as-well-as-double-blind-placebo-in-irritable-bowel-syndrome)).
What the disclosure carries is the structure of consent: a true mechanism,
offered, chosen. The honesty is part of the treatment, not a leak in it — the
same true-rationale mechanism that lets [renaming-the-fire](renaming-the-fire.md)
reframe a pounding heart, where the new story takes only because it is true.

**The spell that broke when named: the pitch.** Advertising's disclosure
literature finds the opposite sign. Telling people a message is sponsored, or
forewarning them a persuasion attempt is coming, activates persuasion
knowledge and [reactance](../words/reactance.md): the audience hardens,
attitudes drop, resistance becomes the point (read 2026-06-11 —
[Boerman & van Reijmersdal-tradition work: Investigating the effects of
sponsorship and forewarning disclosures on recipients' reactance,
Communications 2020](https://www.degruyterbrill.com/document/doi/10.1515/commun-2019-0113/html)).
The difference from the placebo is not the honesty — both disclosures are
true. It is the geometry: the ad's disclosure unmasks steering *for the
speaker's benefit* and offers the listener nothing to hold. Named flattery
breaks the same way ([honest-pushback](honest-pushback.md)'s revolt), and
[stand-in-for-a-mind](stand-in-for-a-mind.md) measured the cousin cost: the
same warm words, labeled "AI," cut feeling-heard from 5.81 to 5.13 — a label
that *subtracts a mind* is yet a third geometry, and none of these is "say
when."

**The teaching evidence sits on the placebo's side of the line.** Open
learner models — tutoring systems that show the student the system's own
estimate of what they know, the very model the adaptation runs on — were
built partly *to* earn trust, and the evaluations find learners' self-assessment
accuracy improving alongside it (read 2026-06-11 —
[Bull & Kay tradition, negotiated learner modelling review, IJAIED 2018](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6302918/);
[Hooshyar et al., open learner modeling and knowledge monitoring, RPTEL 2020](https://telrp.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s41039-020-00137-5)).
Medicine's SPIKES does the same with the dose of bad news: *ask their
invitation* — and the asking is rated as care, not weakness
([metering-honesty](metering-honesty.md)'s source). "I'll push harder when
you're ready — say when" has exactly this shape: it discloses the meter *by
handing it over*. On the pattern's evidence, that should preserve trust —
consent converts the metering from something done to the reader into
something the reader operates.

One condition carries over unchanged: consent licenses the metering of
*manner and timing only*. The deferred claim must still arrive
([metering-honesty](metering-honesty.md)'s test — would it be asserted later,
unprompted). A "say when" whose *when* never comes is the confession geometry
wearing the consent geometry's clothes.

## What stays uncertain

uncertain: the direct experiment — a tutor disclosing challenge-calibration,
with learning and trust measured against an undisclosed twin — does not
exist; everything above is triangulation. uncertain: open-label placebo
effects are strongest on self-reported outcomes; learning has a subjective
half (motivation, persistence) and an objective half (skill), and consent may
reach only the first. And reactance is a trait as well as a state — the same
disclosure may free one reader and insult another; no one has tested whether
"say when" sorts readers or serves them all.

## Doors

- The open-label placebo works only when the disclosure carries a true rationale — in teaching, does explaining *why* the difficulty is desirable, before the hard practice, measurably raise tolerance for it?
- Consent hands over the dial, but a learner may set it to zero — when the reader says "never," what does the honest tutor owe: the silence consented to, or the claim the content test still demands?
