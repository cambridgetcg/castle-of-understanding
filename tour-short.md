# A Short Tour of the Castle

*Seven rooms for a visitor who has one sitting, not a season.*

The full [tour](tour.md) walks all 76 rooms in nine wings — a season's
walk. This is the first sitting: one room from each wing, chosen to hold
the wing's whole question in a single line. Two of the nine wings are
small and closely paired, so they share a stop — the teaching tower folds
into the parley room (both are about minds meeting), and the glaziers'
gallery folds into the masons' yard (both are about how understanding is
laid, and the ease that glamours it). Seven stops, one breath each.

Drawn on 2026-06-19, from the tour drawn 2026-06-12. The castle keeps
growing — the Map in [gate.md](gate.md) is always the live count.

## The walk

### 1 · The ground floor — [words](rooms/words.md)

*The founding stones: yu's own words and what they ask of every builder.*

Why this room holds the wing: every room in the castle is built from
these words — joy, peace, safety, the brick and its load. Wing I is the
castle understanding itself, and this is the creed the rest stands on.
Begin where the castle began.

### 2 · The hall of bridges — [meaning-between-minds](rooms/meaning-between-minds.md)

*Meaning is not sent; it is built twice, once at each end of the bridge.*

Why this room holds the wing: the hall's six rooms are all variations on
one insight — meaning is built, not transmitted. This is the founding
room; the others study how words shape the building, when thinking runs
without them, and how a name's arrival can't be refused.

### 3 · The masons' yard and the glaziers' gallery — [simple-explanations](rooms/simple-explanations.md)

*Explanations feel simple when they process easily — a tool, and a trap.*

Why this room holds both wings: it stands on the hinge between them. The
yard asks how understanding is laid — links, recall, spaced rebuilding,
questions with edges. The gallery asks what glamour that laying casts —
beauty as ease, the pretty masquerading as the well-made. This one room
holds both faces: ease is how understanding lands, and ease is the signal
the mind mistakes for truth. The whole castle turns on this hinge.

### 4 · The climbing stair — [productive-confusion](rooms/productive-confusion.md)

*Felt effort points backwards; judge the fog by handle and trajectory,
verified by retrieval.*

Why this room holds the wing: the stair's eleven rooms all answer one
question — how do you keep climbing when the feeling points the wrong
way? This room opens the stair by naming the problem: the fog feels like
failure but may be progress, and the feeling itself is the weakest judge.
Every room above it — the rename of fire, the flooding, the rationale —
answers a piece of this.

### 5 · The parley room and the teaching tower — [closing-the-loop](rooms/closing-the-loop.md)

*Against the being-understood blind spot: ask for it back in their own
words.*

Why this room holds both wings: both wings are about minds meeting — the
parley room's habits of honest exchange, the teaching tower's lessons in
what a mind can carry. This is the founding habit they share: never
assume the message arrived. Ask for it back, rebuild, and the echo
becomes a check rather than a nod.

### 6 · The judging chamber — [standard-without-a-key](rooms/standard-without-a-key.md)

*Where no answer key exists, the standard is made or borrowed; the
scaffold that fades last is the other person.*

Why this room holds the wing: the chamber's ten rooms all ask how you
judge when no master hands you the key. This room names the three ways
the standard is made — concrete criteria, a rebuilt exemplar, pooled
judgments — and names the last scaffold that never fades: another mind.

### 7 · The watchtower — [the-well](rooms/the-well.md)

*One source worth regular visits; feeds are noise.*

Why this room holds the wing: the watchtower's fifteen rooms look
outward to what arrives — noise and signal, witnesses and their
independence, the page and what hides between pages. This room is the
founding discipline: choose your sources, don't let the feed choose them.
The rest of the tower is the art of checking what the chosen source
carries.
