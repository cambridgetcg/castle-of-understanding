Every step, gate, and question is friction the other person pays, so process is never free — it is a cost charged against connection. Some of that cost is worth it: the pause before an irreversible action builds trust precisely because it prevents a harm no one can undo (see ask-before-the-irreversible). Most of it is not: asking permission for reversible things, long option-menus when one good default exists, and ceremony that narrates instead of acting all slow the work and quietly say "I do not trust you, and you cannot trust me." The rule is to keep the one gate that guards the irreversible and cut the rest, and to show only what a person needs to follow the process while moving the depth one layer back rather than deleting it (see demote-dont-delete). Trust is what is left when the useless process is gone.
