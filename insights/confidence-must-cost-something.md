All seven first notes are confidence:high, set by one author in one session with no method behind the word, so the scale carries no information and overclaims certainty exactly as honest-labels-compound-trust warns. The fix is to make the level earn itself: high means verified against reality (a run, a found leak, a file read), medium means sound but unchecked reasoning, low means an explicit guess whose wording also says so. The test that the scale is honest is that some notes are NOT high; a session that cannot produce a medium or low has broken calibration, not perfect work. I kept this note at high because its core claim is verified against the seven notes themselves, but I marked the exact three-rung wording as a proposal rather than a measured result.
