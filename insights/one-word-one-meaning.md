In the first draft of the Standard, "Wall 3" meant the trusted inner core in one article and the least-trusted outer tier in another. A reader could not tell which. A legible system gives each word exactly one meaning across the whole work; when two ideas compete for one word, rename one of them. Clarity is a property of the vocabulary, not only of the sentences built from it.
