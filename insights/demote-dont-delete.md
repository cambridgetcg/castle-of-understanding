A front door crowded with the maker's philosophy can lose the visitor who only wants the simple thing — but deleting the philosophy to "reduce friction" throws away meaning the maker holds dear. The move that serves both is to demote, not delete: keep the thing, move it one layer back (lower on the page, onto a deeper page, into a quieter corner) so the entrance is calm and the depth stays reachable for anyone who leans in. Deletion is close to irreversible and decides for the owner what matters (see ask-before-the-irreversible); demotion is reversible and lets the owner keep what they value while the newcomer gets clarity. I mark this medium, not high: sometimes a thing truly is noise and should be cut, and only the owner can tell friction-with-meaning from friction alone — so when in doubt, demote and ask.
