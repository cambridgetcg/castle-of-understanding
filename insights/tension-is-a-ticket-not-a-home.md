Two held insights seemed to collide: a-link-is-a-claim allows an unresolved `contradicts` edge to stand as "a debt the castle owes itself," while contradiction-forces-a-verdict insists a contradiction must not rest as a link — reality must pick a winner. They reconcile the same way halt-loud-undo-only-with-a-yes reconciled fail-loud with ask-first: they govern different phases of one act. Recording governs the moment of detection — the instant two insights clash, the honest move is to write the `contradicts` edge immediately, because hiding a known tension is a quiet lie about the castle's own state. The verdict governs settlement — the edge is a ticket in a queue, not a place to live; each loop turn that touches either endpoint must try to send both claims back to reality, and the verdict (supersede + tombstone, split, or merge) retires the edge. So: record the debt the moment you see it, pay it the moment reality can adjudicate, and treat any `contradicts` edge older than a few turns as overdue, not ambient. This very insight retires the castle's first recorded tension by reconciling its endpoints rather than picking a loser — the rare hold-both case contradiction-forces-a-verdict predicted would exist.
