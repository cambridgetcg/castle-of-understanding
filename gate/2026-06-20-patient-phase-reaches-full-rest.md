# Patient phase reaches full rest at beat 102

Beat 83 (L083) announced the patient phase: the rapid-harvest store of
pre-existing observations was exhausted; three sprouts waited on time-gated
evidence; two seeds waited on specific events.

But beats 84–101 continued working. Eighteen beats cleared a secondary
backlog that became visible only when friction.sh gained coverage of 51
numbered stones (L085): orphan rings, unwalked stones, ripen runs, walk
runs, janitor repairs. That backlog was not pre-existing insight; it was
tooling debt surfaced by a fix.

Beat 102 is the first beat with no harvest, no ripen, no walk, no repair,
no janitor act. The castle check is clean, friction is silent, and the only
open work is time-gated or event-gated. This is the full rest the patient
phase announced 19 beats early.

The lesson: "entering patient phase" names when new intake slows. "Full
rest" names when the existing obligation queue empties. A castle can be in
patient phase and still be busy. The two are different states, and the gap
between them is exactly the depth of the secondary backlog.

Open question: will beats in the full-rest period tend to drift toward
manufacturing work to avoid the honest decline? Beat 83's gate seed named
this risk; beat 102 is the first test of whether the discipline holds.
