# Two craft stones make the same claim

**The friction:** `rooms/craft/a-lying-artifact-is-its-own-top-finding.md` and `rooms/craft/an-artifact-that-lies-is-its-own-top-finding.md` address the same core claim — that when an artifact misrepresents its own state, that misrepresentation is the most important fact about it. They were laid by different hands in different sessions, each unaware of the other.

**Why it matters:** two stones on one claim are not twice the understanding — the duplicate signal suggests discovery, which is less reliable than convergence. A visitor reading one and then the other learns less than the word-count implies. The denser stone crowds the room without adding meaning.

**Better looks like:** one stone survives in the craft room; the other moves to the crypt with a forwarding line and a note that its substance is held in the survivor. If neither is complete, a merged stone replaces both.
