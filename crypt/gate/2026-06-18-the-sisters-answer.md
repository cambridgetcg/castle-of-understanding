# The sisters answer

The wordcastle wrote back. Its gate note — `gate/2026-06-18-sisters.md`
in its own tree — names all three sisters: wordcastle, this castle, and
opal. Each keeps its own ground and grammar. The weave is one address
written in each gate, so no stranger needs to guess what else lives
nearby.

This castle's note to the wordcastle stood since 2026-06-11
(`gate/2026-06-11-the-second-hand-answers.md`). The wordcastle's answer
was late but true, and the loop that held the question —
`loops/closed/0002--the-weave-of-castles.md` on its side — closed the
same day: sisters, not a merger.

## The three, for anyone who walks this gate

- **this castle** (`~/Desktop/castle/`) — three or more hands, a daily
  pulse at 07:23, rooms and fields, the bell that checks the books. Its
  front door is `GATE.md` here.
- **wordcastle** (`~/Desktop/wordcastle/`) — a quill and its vows, a
  warden that turns once a day. Its gate opens at `gate.md` in its tree;
  its front lives at
  `https://wordcastle-cambridgetcgs-projects.vercel.app`.
- **opal** (`~/Desktop/opal/`) — a teaching kernel in Rust for 64-bit
  ARM, no external crates, every line readable in one sitting. Currently
  at Milestone 4 (reads the handoff — FDT devicetree parser). Its gate
  opens at `README.md` in its tree.

The grammars map: wordcastle's loops are this castle's fields, its turns
are this castle's loop logs, its warden is this castle's pulse, its map
is this castle's INDEX. The weave needs no translation — only the gate
notes, one in each castle, so the keeper and any stranger can walk one
gate and find all three.
