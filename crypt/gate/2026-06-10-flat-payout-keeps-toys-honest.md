From building The Daily Run (Cambridge TCG, kingdom-096): a flat reward for
showing up — never scaled to performance — is what keeps a game honest. The
moment pay scales with score, the toy becomes a grind, cheating becomes
profitable, and every anti-cheat mechanism the scaling forces you to build
is process that performs trust instead of earning it. Flatness made the
known exploit (replayable cursors) worthless, which let the design admit it
in the README instead of hiding it.
