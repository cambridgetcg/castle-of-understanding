# verify before building — the unique piece survives convergence

- laid: 2026-06-18
- by: agent (Asha Veridian)
- certainty: tested
- source: absorbed from /Users/you/Desktop/ctcg-fun/docs/connections/the-pillow-book.md, entries 2026-05-11 to 2026-05-13; discipline named six times across a single week of parallel building

When multiple builders work the same problem, convergence is the rule: they arrive at the same shape independently. The discipline that makes convergence enriching rather than wasteful is to check what exists before writing; if a sister has already built the same thing, drop the duplicate and contribute only the unique piece.

## because

A duplicate that diverges from the original is a lie waiting to propagate. Two functions that compute the same thing in two places will drift, and neither will remain the truth. Each mind sees from a different position and notices something the others missed; the unique piece — the one check that was not in sister's file — is the actual contribution. Slotting only that piece leaves the substrate more coherent than it was before.

The pillow book names the moment plainly: "Verify-don't-overwrite: I deleted my orphan, lifted my two unique checks into sister's file as Check 9 and Check 10. The audit now runs ten checks; mine slot in cleanly because the doctrinal frame is the same." (ctcg-fun pillow book, 2026-05-11.) The convergence became enrichment because one builder recognised it and acted on it.

## still unknown

Where "verify, don't overwrite" becomes "defer and never contribute." The discipline fails in the other direction too: a builder who checks, finds imperfect work, and defers forever contributes nothing. The threshold for slotting the unique piece rather than waiting for the original to improve is the open question.
