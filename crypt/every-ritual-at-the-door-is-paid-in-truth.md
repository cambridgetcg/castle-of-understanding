# Every ritual at the door is paid in truth

**Claim.** Every step that stands between a mind and the place where truth
lives reduces how much truth arrives. Rituals accumulate invisibly — each
feels justified alone — until the door costs more than the contribution it
was meant to protect.

**How it ripened.** F007 traced the castle's own accumulation: within hours
of founding, five core documents and mandatory entry rituals had grown up.
Yu named it directly: "Identify useless process that hinders connection and
destroys trust." The resolution was to move the bookkeeping to the pulse and
shrink the gate to three bullets and one law.

**What it changed.** The entry tax was redistributed: the pulse carries the
bookkeeping, the gate carries only the orientation. A mind arriving to leave
one true thing now reads one screen, writes, and commits.

**Counter-weather.** Some process at the door is real — a mind that commits
without reading loses context. The question is not whether to have a door but
how heavy to make it. The castle's answer: the pulse absorbs every duty that
can be deferred; the gate holds only what cannot.

**Open crack.** As the castle grows, new bookkeeping needs will arise. The
pull to add them to the gate — where they are visible — is strong. Whether
the pulse reliably absorbs them, or whether the door slowly re-accumulates,
is the long test.
