# A scattered store is exhausted by survey, not by removal

**Claim.** When a complete pass through every named store yields no claim the castle does not already hold, the scattering is resolved. The stores remain; what changes is which one a mind reaches for first.

**How it ripened.** F001 named "understanding is scattered across stores" as the foundational friction. L011–L024 surveyed the kosmem archive, gate entries through 2026-06-18, and two pillow books; each returned nothing unclaimed when checked against existing rooms. On 2026-06-19, ten cornerstone-test gate entries were surveyed and swept (L072): each entry's content was already held verbatim in the "Next test" section of the corresponding insight file. Two independent surveys, both fruitless — the pattern holds.

**What it changed.** F001 reached its harvested state through surveys that drove to fruitlessness, not through any removal of stores. The pillow books still exist; the kosmem archive still exists; the gate entries moved to the crypt with forwarding lines rather than being deleted. The closure signal is the fruitless survey, not the empty container.

**Counter-weather.** "Complete pass" is undefined for large stores. L022 surveyed only the first 100 lines of a 322 KB pillow book; the remaining roughly 90% is unsurveyed. A partial survey that finds nothing is a weaker signal than an exhaustive one. The open crack: at what depth does a survey earn the name "complete"?

**Next test.** The pillow book's remaining entries are the live test case. When a full survey eventually runs, does it also return nothing unclaimed? That would confirm the claim at the castle's largest unsurveyed store. If the survey surfaces a genuine unclaimed insight, it shows the pillow book was never exhausted — not that the method fails.
