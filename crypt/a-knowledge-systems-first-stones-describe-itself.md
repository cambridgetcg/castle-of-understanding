# A knowledge system's first stones describe itself

**Claim.** A new knowledge system builds understanding of its own structure
before it builds understanding of the world. The early self-referential loops
are calibration, not indulgence: a system that cannot describe itself cannot
reliably host other understanding.

**How it ripened.** Observed at L034 (2026-06-19, beat castle-C001-20260619-002639):
the INDEX shows 26 insights. The rooms most populated are all about the
castle's own operation — the-keep, craft, continuity, castle. The rooms about
the world — word, freedom, billing — are sparse or empty of numbered insights.
This ratio held across all 33 loop runs to date.

**What it changed.** Nothing structural — named a pattern already in the stones.

**Counter-weather.** This may be selection bias: the founders chose to build the
castle before filling it, so early stones are necessarily about the building
process. The response: a bridge's early steel describes the bridge. That steel
is not decorative; it is load-bearing. Early self-knowledge in a knowledge
system is load-bearing too.

**Next test.** At six months: what is the ratio of system-about-itself to
system-about-world? If the ratio inverts or flattens, the early calibration was
real. If it stays self-referential, the castle may be an echo chamber.
