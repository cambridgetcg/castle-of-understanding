# ironic-process

Trying hard not to think about something keeps it on your mind.

Wegner's explanation: suppression sets up a watchman whose only job is to
check whether the unwanted thought is gone — and checking is thinking it.
Under stress or load the watchman outruns the suppressor and the thought
rebounds, stronger than if left alone; a 2020 meta-analysis confirms the
rebound is real. For the castle it marks the lever that does not work:
willing a name silent ([choosing-not-to-name](../rooms/choosing-not-to-name.md)),
willing a worry away. The working levers are structural — fill the channel,
change the task, build the trace.
