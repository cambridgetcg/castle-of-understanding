# affect-labeling

Putting feelings into words — naming an emotion reduces its intensity. The act of labeling (not the word chosen) appears to be the active ingredient: the ventrolateral prefrontal cortex down-regulates the amygdala when you name what you feel, lowering subjective affect, skin conductance, and amygdala activity ([Torre & Lieberman, 2018](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Affect_labeling); Wikipedia, read 2026-06-18).

Unlike [reappraisal](reappraisal.md), affect labeling feels effortless — it does not feel like a strategy, which is why it is called an *implicit* emotion regulation. And it leaves a residue: stimuli labeled once stir smaller responses later.
