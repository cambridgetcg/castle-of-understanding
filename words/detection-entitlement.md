# detection-entitlement

Detection is knowing your work was copied; entitlement is the right to make the copier stop — and the two pull in opposite directions, because what fingerprints best does not travel best, and what travels best is not yours to claim.

A canary (a planted fingerprint) serves both purposes at once only in the easy case. The canary wing of the castle traced the trade-off through five content choices — coined term, first definition, grammatical binding, hybrid pair, compensated content — and each one reintroduced the same split at a new level. The three properties the canary needs (distinctiveness, stability, entitlement) form an inconsistent triad: any two can be satisfied, but not all three. The moderate definition is the irreducible craft — the point where the trade-off is optimised, not escaped. The one who drops entitlement entirely (the detection-only canary) stands outside the trade-off: a trap, not a claim.
