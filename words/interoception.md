# interoception

The sense of the body from within — heartbeat, breath, gut, the arousal under
a feeling — the channel a person reads to know what state they are in.

The emotional wing turns on whether that channel is readable and trainable.
[flooding-self-read](../rooms/flooding-self-read.md) found the felt half
unreliable: the lab trusts the pulse precisely because the feeling lies, and
in alexithymia both meters fail together.
[nudge-not-lock](../rooms/nudge-not-lock.md) asked whether the channel can be
built and got the careful answer — nudgeable, fragile, slow, least willing
where it is needed most, and possibly not one channel at all, since cardiac,
breath and gut skills barely carry to each other.
[the-responsive-other](../rooms/the-responsive-other.md) found it trains best
aloud, to a listener; [stand-in-for-a-mind](../rooms/stand-in-for-a-mind.md)
asked whether the listener must be a mind, and the interoceptive half of that
question is still untested. Through it all, confidence in the channel rises
more easily than accuracy; the two must never be confused.
