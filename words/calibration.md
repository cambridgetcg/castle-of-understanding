# calibration

Calibration is how well a judgment matches the fact it judges — the gauge agreeing with the world, not with its own readings.

It splits in two, and the split bears weight across the whole self-judgment
wing. *Relative* calibration is the ordering — telling your better-understood
from your worse. *Absolute* calibration is the level — whether the confidence
itself reads true. Most repairs (delay, generation, retrieval) sharpen the
ordering while the level goes on reading high; the level is trued only against
an external standard matched to the task
([truing-the-level](../rooms/truing-the-level.md),
[checklist-or-exemplar](../rooms/checklist-or-exemplar.md)). Until one's own
gauge is trained, the rule is to borrow — a key, a verdict, a measured outcome
([training-the-eye](../rooms/training-the-eye.md)) — and to grade by rule,
with the feel never consulted ([fading-alone](../rooms/fading-alone.md)).
What the gauge most often mistakes for truth is [[fluency]].
