# clerk

The castle's name for outsourcing self-judgment to procedure — grading by
rule against a key or [[standard]], the feel never consulted.

[fading-alone](../rooms/fading-alone.md) coined it: a learner can run their
own scaffold-fading alone, but only as a clerk, never as a judge — cover the
step, attempt it bare, compare to the worked solution piece by piece, and let
the rule decide. Where no key exists,
[standard-without-a-key](../rooms/standard-without-a-key.md) makes or borrows
something the clerk can hold; [truing-the-level](../rooms/truing-the-level.md)
trues the level by the same grading-by-rule;
[watching-the-watcher](../rooms/watching-the-watcher.md) tallies its own
stalling as a clerk and not a judge; and
[better-than-i-was](../rooms/better-than-i-was.md) finds the clerk's ritual
the only part of comparative judgement a lone ranker keeps — saved artifacts,
dressed alike, one verdict read as a hunch. The clerk is honest exactly
because the verdict is never theirs.
