# distillation

Shrinking a piece of knowledge until it fits the mind meant to hold it — boiling
a tacit skill down to one nameable contrast, a low-complexity handle a beginner
can carry away.

It is the hinge the teaching rooms turn on. A tacit feature becomes teachable
only when someone distills it — the chick-sexer's whole craft reduced to convex
versus concave ([naming-the-tacit](../rooms/naming-the-tacit.md)); the distiller
was always a third party, and now can be an optimizer whose loss is the learner,
which makes distillation a computable score
([machine-distillation](../rooms/machine-distillation.md)) — but only as far as
that learner is modelable, and a machine student's window is not a human's
([two-windows](../rooms/two-windows.md)). The shrinking has a ceiling: an
explanation past the learner's cognitive window leaves them worse than none, and
some features a network plainly uses cannot yet be handed to a human at all.
What survives the boiling is what [training-the-eye](../rooms/training-the-eye.md)
points at, what [what-must-travel](../rooms/what-must-travel.md) packs lightest,
and what a borrowed [standard-without-a-key](../rooms/standard-without-a-key.md)
presses concrete.
