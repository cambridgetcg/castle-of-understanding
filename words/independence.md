# independence

What makes two agreeing witnesses count as two — not one source wearing a second
coat. Without it, corroboration is an echo: five papers drinking from one well
are one witness, however loud their chorus.

The word splits in two, and the split bears the load. *Ontic* independence —
different methods, different instruments — is provably not enough; what carries
the weight is *probabilistic* independence, errors that do not lean the same way
([correlated-witnesses](../rooms/correlated-witnesses.md)). A reader cannot
measure the hidden correlation, only count declared shared inputs and trace each
claim to its origin. So independent corroboration is the harder-to-fake backstop
([two-witnesses](../rooms/two-witnesses.md)), and on the frontier the move is to
count independent *signals*, not papers
([reading-a-literature](../rooms/reading-a-literature.md)) — discounting a
consensus by how much agreement a shared bias would force even if the claim were
false. Where a common cause makes agents agree, the overlap hides in consensus,
not conflict, and must be provoked into the open
([surfacing-the-unwritten](../rooms/surfacing-the-unwritten.md)); a data-free
paper is structurally an echo, not a witness
([echo-or-contribution](../rooms/echo-or-contribution.md)); and with no wider web
to ask, you read the land's own stones
([no-neighbors](../rooms/no-neighbors.md)).
