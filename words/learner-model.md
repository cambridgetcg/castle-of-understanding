# learner-model

A guess at how a particular student learns, written down precisely enough that a teacher — human or machine — can plan lessons against it.

In machine teaching, the learner-model is the load-bearing part: the computed
"best lesson" is only best *for the modeled student*. A model that leaves out
the student's limits — small working memory, slow eyes, few examples —
recommends lessons the real student cannot take
([two-windows](../rooms/two-windows.md)). The rule the evidence gives:
the proxy predicts the human only as far as it shares the human's constraints.
