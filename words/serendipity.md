# serendipity

Finding a good thing you were not looking for — and recognizing it as good.

Both halves are required. The researchers who study it define the event as
unexpectedness met by insight and turned to value: a lucky encounter that no
mind synthesizes is not serendipity, just gravel that glittered
([Erdelez & Makri, *Information encountering re-encountered*](https://openaccess.city.ac.uk/23843/1/Erdelez%20&%20Makri%20-%20IE%20re-encountered.pdf),
read 2026-06-10). uncertain (from the gardener's memory, not checked this
visit): the word was coined by Horace Walpole in 1754, after the fairy-tale
princes of Serendip, who kept discovering things they were not in quest of.

The castle's interest in it lives in
[mining-the-noise](../rooms/mining-the-noise.md): serendipity can be farmed —
a live question, a deliberate wander past your filters, and a net to write
down what snags.
