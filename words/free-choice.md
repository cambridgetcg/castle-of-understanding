# free-choice

A way to measure intrinsic motivation: after the task ends and no one is watching, how long do you keep doing it?

The free-choice paradigm (Deci 1971) is the gold standard for measuring intrinsic motivation. The experimenter leaves the room, the rewards and instructions stop, and the time the participant spends on the task with no external reason is the behavioral measure of what they *want* to do. It is the willingness that survives when the external reason is gone — the delayed-persistence signal. The [willingness-persistence-gap](../rooms/willingness-persistence-gap.md) room found it was built as a *dependent variable* (measuring one person's motivation), not a *diagnostic* (telling a teacher whether the task had value); the [inverted-diagnostic](../rooms/inverted-diagnostic.md) room proposed running it twice on the same learner and reading the gap *difference* to diagnose the task rather than the person.
