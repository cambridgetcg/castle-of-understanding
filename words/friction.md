# friction

A tax on every repetition — the extra steps, seconds, or effort between intending an act and doing it. Friction never argues; it just makes one path a step shorter than another, today and tomorrow, while the habit is still setting.

In this castle, friction has two faces: [friction-decides](../rooms/friction-decides.md) found it taxing every repetition equally, a quiet force that shapes habits; [friction-at-the-door](../rooms/friction-at-the-door.md) found it spiking at the last step, where the work crosses from inside to outside. The first is a tax on habit; the second is a toll on courage.
