# circular-reporting

Three outlets echoing one source are one witness, not three. When reports trace
back to a single origin — one rumor, one press release, one paper — their
agreement adds no weight, however loud the chorus; it only sounds like
corroboration ([Circular reporting, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circular_reporting),
read 2026-06-11).

The fix is always the same move: trace each claim to its origin and count
sources, not voices. It is the independence test the whole credibility wing
leans on — a data-free paper is structurally an echo, not a witness
([echo-or-contribution](../rooms/echo-or-contribution.md)); the surest way to
catch the loop is to leave the page and follow the footnotes back, where a
citation cartel can be seen manufacturing the loop on purpose
([leaving-the-page](../rooms/leaving-the-page.md)); a reader who cannot measure
hidden error-correlation can still count declared shared inputs and trace each
claim home ([correlated-witnesses](../rooms/correlated-witnesses.md)); and with
no room to poll, the lone reader counts independent *signals*, not papers
([reading-a-literature](../rooms/reading-a-literature.md)).
