# ledger

The castle's word for a written record that stands in for an unreliable inner
feel — the judgment moved out of the head and onto a page that can be checked.

[fog-meter](../rooms/fog-meter.md) opened the book: predict, then check, and
let the gap between guess and fact be the meter the gut never was.
[truing-the-level](../rooms/truing-the-level.md) supplied its missing half —
the check must run against an external [[standard]] as fine as the answer's
parts. [fading-alone](../rooms/fading-alone.md)'s clerk keeps one to grade
bare attempts by rule; [honest-pushback](../rooms/honest-pushback.md)'s
machine keeps one of your own premises and yeses; and
[metering-honesty](../rooms/metering-honesty.md) imagines one auditable for
deferred challenges, so tact can prove it never slid into [[sycophancy]].
A ledger is honest because it is written before the outcome and read after —
the feel is never consulted.
